Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (For College Students in India)

1.  Use strong and unique passwords
    Create passwords using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid
    using the same password for multiple accounts.

2.  Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
    Always turn on 2FA for important accounts like email, social media,
    and banking apps for extra security.

3.  Beware of phishing links
    Do not click on suspicious links received through email, SMS, or
    WhatsApp. Always verify the sender before taking any action.

4.  Keep your devices updated
    Regularly update your phone, laptop, and apps to protect against
    security vulnerabilities.

5.  Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities
    Do not access banking apps or personal accounts using unsecured
    public Wi-Fi networks.

6.  UPI Safety Tip: Never share your UPI PIN
    No bank or payment app will ever ask for your UPI PIN. Sharing it
    can lead to immediate financial loss.

7.  UPI Safety Tip: Verify before you pay
    Always check the receiver’s name before approving any payment. Be
    cautious of “collect requests” asking you to send money.

8.  Download apps only from trusted sources
    Install apps only from official platforms like Google Play Store or
    Apple App Store. Avoid unknown APK files.

9.  Limit sharing of personal information
    Avoid sharing sensitive details like phone number, address, or
    college ID publicly online.

10. Always log out from shared devices
    If using a cyber café or shared computer, make sure to log out of
    all accounts after use.

Report Cyber Crimes

If you face any cyber fraud or suspicious activity, report it
immediately:

National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
Helpline Number: 1930

Early reporting can help prevent further damage and increase the chances
of recovery.
