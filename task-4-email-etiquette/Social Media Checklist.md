# Responsible Social Media Use for College Students

A practical checklist to help you navigate social media thoughtfully during your college years.

✅ DO's


 1. Think before you post ask yourself if you'd be comfortable with a professor, parent, or future employer seeing it because  your digital footprint follows you into the job market .
 2. Use privacy settings on all platforms to control who sees your content it limits exposure to strangers and unwanted audiences. 
 3. Take regular breaks and set screen time limits Protects your mental health and academic focus 
 4. Verify information before sharing check credible sources first prevents you from spreading misinformation.
 5. Support and uplift peers by engaging positively with their content builds a healthy, encouraging campus community.


❌ DON'Ts

 1. Don't post in anger or distress step away and cool down first Emotional posts can damage relationships and reputations 
 2. Don't share personal details publicly (address, schedule, ID numbers) Protects your physical safety and identity 
 3. Don't plagiarize or misrepresent others content without credit it's unethical and can have academic legal consequences 
 4. Don't engage with or amplify cyberbullying report it instead it keeps online spaces safer for everyone 
 5. Don't use social media as your only news source algorithms create echo chambers and may distort your worldview.


 Quick rule of thumb: If it could embarrass you, harm someone, or compromise your safety — don't post it. Your online presence is an extension of your character. 🎓
